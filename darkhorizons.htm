    IN SUMMARY - Robert Smith?
     For a 300 page novel, this zooms by. You'd think that a tale of Vikings and islanders would be dullness itself, but this book manages to keep it lively. There are some richly drawn characters and some genuinely touching moments that wouldn't have nearly the power they do without some serious character work that's gone into them. Henrik being more impressed with the outside of the TARDIS than the interior is laugh-out-loud hilarious. The aliens are refreshingly different. There's some genuinely impressive writing, such as the sequence in the cave, with the fourth Doctor, Leela and K9 cameo. And the Doctor is very well done too, being a mix of gabbling idiot, lonely god, ineffectual and powerful. But what year does this take place in? The book never bothers to tell us.
