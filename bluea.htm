    BASIC PLOT
     The Doctor becomes involved in the plotting of Deadalus, an enormous Jade elephant, who intends to provoke war between the Galactic Federation and a hitherto unseen side universe known as the Obverse. Meanwhile another, different, Doctor - whose mother is a mermaid - lives in a house with his companions Fitz and Compassion, and cooks dinner for his friend Sally and her dog, Canine. Meanwhile, giant owls attack Iris Wildthyme in a shopping centre somewhere, as the snows come and winter falls. And this Christmas, winter is bitter indeed.

    PREPARATORY READING
     It's an interesting book as, in many ways, it deliberately reflects aspects of Interference, but that's not vital. Lots of references are made to The Scarlet Empress.

    CONTINUITY REFERENCES
     Pg 1 "But I want to check on the garden." The Doctor talks directly to the audience, as he did in The Scarlet Empress, although it's much less irritating this time, because he's a) seemingly unaware that he's doing it and b) infinitely less smug.

    Pg 3 "The seventh son of a seventh son." This is what, many moons ago, made you a sorcerer. It's not meant literally for the Doctor, no matter how many people have taken it to be so.

    IN SUMMARY - Anthony Wilson
     Well, it's much, much better than The Scarlet Empress, perhaps because the subject matter is so much darker this time and, although Magrs would probably prefer it otherwise, he's very good at dark. The sections with the Obverse (we presume) Doctor are hypnotic in intensity and quality and they're a thrill to read. Nothing is explained and, while we appreciate the throw-away nature of the cod Trek sequences, we'd still have liked some sort of conclusion, but then we'd have like a conclusion to the story about the Time Lord/Enemy War and we never got one, so this seems like small fry by comparision. The writing's charming and often quite funny, and Iris is a lot less irritating than she was last time. Compassion's great and Fitz, although not really in it much, is quite fun too. The Doctor almost takes control and, yeah, we were happy with that. There is one gripe, though: while it's all very clever and all, we've still damn all idea about what happened. Oh, and the questions at the end manage to somehow be simultaneously clever, annoying and patronising. So it loses marks for that too.
