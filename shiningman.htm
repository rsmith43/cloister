    IN SUMMARY - Robert Smith?
     You know what? That wasn't half bad. It's a decent thriller, although it doesn't quite seem to know whether it wants to be a sub-Stranger Things horror story set in the woods or a treatise on the nature of internet memes. Marrying the two together is awkward, especially the final scene, which needs to have consequences, rather than a cliffhanger. But the Doctor is well done, even if Bill is pretty generic, and the menace is pretty spooky at times. Differentiating between the Fairies, the Boggarts (who are also called fairies at one point) and the Shining Men (who are also Boggarts) will have you scratching your head for no good reason. But, overall, aside from some first novel-itis, this is a decent final book in the Capaldi run, which has been a quantum level of quality above that of any New Series Doctor (excepting the War Doctor). Not half bad at all.
