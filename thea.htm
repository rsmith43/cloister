Theatre of War
by Justin Richards

Publisher: Virgin
ISBN: 0 426 20414 X

     

    BASIC PLOT
     The Doctor and Ace finds themselves on the dead planet Menaxus, which is being excavated by an archeological team led by Lannic. Lannic hails from Heletia, a nearby warring planet obsessed with stage plays. They discover the Dream Machine, a holographic device that creates virtual plays. While Bernice investigates the disappearance of the Menaxan civilisation at the Braxiatel library, the Doctor and Ace use the Dream Machine to try and avert war. But is all really as it seems?

    IN SUMMARY - Robert Smith?
     This is one of the best and cleverest novels the NAs produced, and criminally overlooked. The entire thing is literate, clever and subtle, with a glorious master plan engineered by Braxiatel, a stupendous plot twist in the nature of Menaxus and the brilliant suggestion that Lannic was a construct of the machine all along. The writing is crisp, the source documents and quotes are excellent (with their own brilliant twist that Benny recognises that they're fake for the fact that they had to be written by a single author - which would be Richards himself) and the regulars (plus Braxiatel) very vivid. Oh, and it single-handedly creates the backdrop for the Benny adventures. It's a shame that this isn't better remembered, because it's stupendous.
