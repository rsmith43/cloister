    IN SUMMARY - Robert Smith?
     Simply stunning. The time-travel antics are brilliantly done, and a worthy precursor to Steven Moffat. The idea of the Beautiful Death is a neat one, while there are fun side things such as the perpetually hungry spiders. Having a single time jump would have worked nicely. Having a second makes you smile. By the time you're on the third, with overlapping Doctors, Romanas and K9s all just staying out of each other's way, you're grinning ear to ear. But it's more than just clever ideas: the characterisation is absolutely spot on for all three regulars, with funny dialogue, introspective observations and running gags. ERIC the depressed computer should have "Property of D. Adams" stamped on him somewhere. And the way the pieces interlock to form a beautiful and tragic plot is just sensational. One of the best Doctor Who books ever written.
