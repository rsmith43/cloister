    CONTINUITY COCK-UPS

    1. Pg 5 "They had met, at last, in orbit around Proxima II" Strangely, in The Face-Eater, Messingham called the planet Proxima 2, not Proxima II.
    2. Pg 34 "I looked back just to check Fitz was there and then it all becomes a bit of blur." Say what? "A bit of a blur", surely?
    3. Pg 38 "Oddly, Fitz was winning. He'd never won a fight in his life." This isn't true, he beat the Masked Weasel easily in The Crooked World (pg 70).
    4. Pg 64 There's a giant sea monster, despite there being no native life on Selonart (pg 14). It's never explained where it came from.
    5. Pg 70 "It wasn't working out quite as he had anticipated. he hadn't realised this Doctor could be the most annoying person in the galaxy." Huh? "He hadn't", surely?
    6. Pg 81 "And that's who he reminded me of all of a sudden. Gene Wilder. As Willie Wonka in the film." Except that the film (and book) involved "Willy Wonka", so who's this mysterious Willie?
    7. Pg 101 "I thought about leaping into the sea but had to admit I was too tired to be bothered. Where could I go then?" Only down, since you can't swim, Fitz. This is mentioned in both EarthWorld (pg 194) and Reckless Engineering (pg 114).
    8. Pg 130 "The Governer hoped he would n ever have to see them ever again." Pardon? "Would never", surely?
    9. Pg 134 "What makes you think its in one piece anyway?" Or that "it's" in one piece?

    PLUGGING THE HOLES [Fan-wank theorizing of how to fix continuity cock-ups]

    1. During the early days of the colony it was "Proxima 2", but since then it's grown more confident and austere and retitled itself using Roman numerals to reflect this.
    2. It's such a blur that Anji starts forgetting her words.
    3. Anji didn't witness that one and refuses to believe any bragging Fitz may have done about it.
    4. It's from an alternate universe and it breaking through via the timebergs.
    5. Peck is so annoyed by the Doctor that he's forgetting to capitalise the first letter of his sentences.
    6. Anji's Freudian subconscious is telling us more than we want to know about her interest in the Doctor.
    7. Fitz is really desperate to escape and hoping that the Selonart water might help him out, like swimming in the Dead Sea.
    8. The Governer is so hopeful that it's giving him a weird stutter.
    9. Warner is so convinced it's not in one piece that he's using the possessive, not the contraction. He's very eccentric.

    IN SUMMARY - Robert Smith?
     The first three quarters of The Infinity Race is great. Not perfect -- the sea monster is never explained, Sabbath's disguise is so obvious that you expect the twist to be that it isn't actually him and a lot more could have been done with the idea of an alternate universe where Sabbath was public enemy number 1 -- but it rolls along nicely. There's some nice writing, a convincing setup and the title turns out to be really clever. But then the last quarter is abysmally poor. Sabbath becomes a ranting villain, there's technobabble everywhere and the whole thing slows to a crawl (although the time loop rewriting is fun). In the end, this leaves an extremely bitter taste in the mouth, which it probably doesn't fully deserve. A shame.
