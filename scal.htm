The Scales of Injustice
by Gary Russell

Publisher: Virgin
ISBN: 0 426 20477 8

     

    BASIC PLOT
     A rash of strange occurrences leads the Doctor to believe another group of Silurians is awakening. Liz Shaw joins forces with a reporter to investigate reports of mysterious cave men, while the Brigadier struggles with reduced funding for UNIT and the break-up of his first marriage.

    DOCTOR
     Third.

    COMPANIONS
     Liz Shaw, the Brigadier, Mike Yates, Sergeant Benton.

    MATERIALISATION CIRCUIT
     None. The TARDIS spends the story in the Doctor's laboratory (page 21).

    PREPARATORY READING
     This is a sequel to Doctor Who and the Silurians. (As always, this means references to its prequel are not individually noted below.)

    CONTINUITY COCK-UPS

    1. "Her most recent assignment had pitted her against an alien for not only far away - the tropics - but, via the Doctor's 'space-time visualizer', back and forth in time as well." In The Chase, this is called the "Time and Space visualiser" not space-time visualiser.
    2. On page 94 the blond man has beaten his wife to death. On page 96, he shot her through the chin.
    3. Pg 116 "'It involved Smallmarshes, a seaside town near Hastings. Near Kent.' 'I know where Hastings is Corporal.'" Hastings is in Sussex.
    4. Pg 227 Carol Bell briefly becomes Maisie Bell.
    5. Pg 232 Captain Hawkins' first name has changed to Stan (from Sam on page 182).
    6. Pg 232 Maisie Hawke has become Maisie Hawkes.
    7. Pg 242 "Over there is George Hibbert" Hibbert was atomized in Spearhead from Space, so he can't have any remains.

    PLUGGING THE HOLES [Fan-wank theorizing of how to fix continuity cock-ups]

    1. Liz is simply continuing her error from Eye of the Giant.
    2. His wife was already dead when he shot her.
    3. Maisie Hawke and the Brigadier are using a secret code in case anyone is evesdropping on their phone call.
    4. Corporal Bell's full name is Carol Maisie Bell.
    5. Captain Hawkins has the unlikely full name of Sam Stan Hawkins.
    6. Maisie Hawke has the even more unlikely full name of Maisie Hawkes-Hawke. It's probably lucky she didn't marry Captain Hawkins.
    7. C19 took the wrong body.

    IN SUMMARY - Robert Smith?
     I liked this book. I liked it a lot better when it was called Who Killed Kennedy, but that's by the by. There's an attempt to provoke atmosphere by simply drowning the book in early Pertwee continuity and hoping that'll pass muster... and oddly enough it pretty much does. The hybrid Silurians are a fun idea, the Brigadier's marriage breakdown is genuinely touching and there's a lot of good work done with Liz in the first of her multiple departures. The book's internal continuity leaks all over the place, but otherwise this is passable enough fare, with a few moments that really stand out.
