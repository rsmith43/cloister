Shakedown
by Terrance Dicks

Publisher: Virgin
ISBN: 0 426 20459 X

     

    BASIC PLOT
     Two incarnations after the event, the Doctor picks up the trail of the Rutan who escaped in Lords of the Storm. While the Doctor chats to the Rutan collectorate, Chris and Roz chase down a serial killer and Benny gets sent off to somewhere allegedly safe. Meanwhile, the good ship Tiger Moth is setting off on its Shakedown cruise...

    DOCTOR
     Seventh.

    COMPANIONS
     Chris, Roz and Benny.

    MATERIALISATION CIRCUIT
     The TARDIS materializes on Jekkar before the book begins.

     Lords of the Storm is useful, but actually mostly tangential. It's certainly not vital.

    CONTINUITY COCK-UPS

    1. On Pg 38, Roz and Chris' response to Garshak brandishing the Adjudicator's motto has two faults. Firstly, Roz and Chris should have responded with 'And fairness be your friend.' Here they omit the 'And'. Furthermore, how stupid have they suddenly become? They've gone to great lengths to create the perfect cover story, and they both blow it without a moment's thought. One of them, maybe, just about, would be believable, but both? I thought they were trained! Roz has been an Adjudicator for over 20 years!
    2. Pg 41 "No, more like Eridanean Brandy." Except that, in both Deceit (pg 100) and Terrance's own Blood Harvest (pg 32), it was Eridanian Brandy.
    3. Pg 223 "'Hyperspace travel is almost instantaneous,' said the Doctor." Really? It wasn't in Nightmare of Eden. Nor, rather more tellingly, was it instantaneous for either the Hyperion or the Tiger Moth in the course of this very book.

    IN SUMMARY - Anthony Wilson
     Could have been terrible but actually bounces along nicely and comes to a quite dramatically satisfying conclusion. It's nice that the TARDIS crew get to do the yacht race - a little bit of downtime for them. The structure is startlingly similar to that of Blood Harvest, but thankfully without decamping to Gallifrey at the end. There is the odd unintentionally funny bit, such as the portrayal of downtown Megacity which comes across as somewhere Terrance has never been but would really like to go. The best bit by far, though, is Steg's glorious decision to make everyone look the other way while the Doctor tampers with the machine at the end. Rarely has disbelief been so suspended. But it's a fun ride, with some good characters and some nice touches. Just don't expect a great deal of depth.
