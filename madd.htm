Publisher: BBC

    BASIC PLOT

    CONTINUITY COCK-UPS

    1. Pg 23 "I was hanging around this place in Kent [...] I had a house near there for a while, you see" The Doctor's house on Allen Road is near Kent. It's not clear if this is the same one, as the eighth Doctor wouldn't remember that he owns it.
    2. Pg 55 "Cleavis would beam and clap Tyler on the shoulder and murmur: 'Well, would you be without him, old fella?'" Where, surely?
    3. Pg 148 "And the Doctor soon lost track of everyone's names." Really? The Doctor?!?
    4. Pg 163 "Talk to him the only language he understands" Say what?
    5. Pg 209 "'Web of time?' smiled Coward. 'Oh, really, Doctor. Do you really believe that semi-mystical nonsense?'" Except that Noel himself seems to believe it on page 214: "'Are you telling me that I did quite the wrong thing, in helping you to spring chaos upon the unsuspecting web of time?' Noel demanded."
    6. Pg 247 "But this means... I'm not alone!" Except that the Doctor already knew he wasn't alone, having met the Master at his wedding, in The Adventuress of Henrietta Street, where he was also told that there were four Time Lords left.

    PLUGGING THE HOLES [Fan-wank theorizing of how to fix continuity cock-ups]

    1. The house might have properties that draw the Doctor to it and prevent other Doctors from visiting if one is already there.
    2. Cleavis had clearly had quite a bit to drink by the time he did this.
    3. The Doctor is extremely focussed on his mission and has no need to remember names, so he doesn't bother.
    4. The only language he understands is Tarzan's language.
    5. Noel must have been convinced by the Doctor's response, despite his apparently not being so at the time.
    6. The Doctor's overexcited by actually seeing a TARDIS dematerialise, so he's having a momentary memory lapse.

    IN SUMMARY - Robert Smith?
     A book you really can judge by its cover. Mad Dogs and Englishman takes a little while to get going, but it's hilarious stuff. It doesn't matter at all that the plot's a bit loopy and the Doctor only affects the outcome by forgetting something important, this is another laugh-riot from the twisted mind of Paul Magrs. If you think the cover's funny, you'll love it; otherwise steer clear, it's probably not for you. But I adored it.
