    BASIC PLOT
     The Doctor takes Sarah Jane to meet one of her heroes, Rudyard Kipling. But when the TARDIS materializes, they discover a series of deadly experiments are being carried out to interfere with human evolution.

    CONTINUITY COCK-UPS

    1. Pg 2 "He'd been shot at once, and in the stormy weather he could still feel the ache from the shot." So was he shot or shot at?
    2. Pg 13 "Despite the strains of music from inside the Great Hall, there was un unmistakeable sound of a howl in the air." Say what?
    3. Pg 16 "Sarah Jane Smith lay on her back in what the Doctor had dismissingly referred to as 'the bath' and lazily stirred her hands and feet. She couldn't remember the last time she wore a swimsuit." Except that the last time she wore a swimsuit, in Death to the Daleks, she pointed out that she couldn't swim and, in fact, could sink anywhere.
    4. Pg 73 "'You will all return for dinner, I trust,?' asked Fullbright." Why is there a comma before the question mark?
    5. Pg 89 "A whaling ship stops off here instead of Scotland, barely hours before a man is killed and its irrelevant?" Its irrelevent what?
    6. Pg 119 "'I'm sure you're a blithering idiot,' Sir Edmund grunted." Why does Sir Edward become Sir Edmund briefly? Especially since that's actually the name of Colonel Edmund Ross.
    7. Pg 124 "What is this world coming to? Women shooting and hunting and now me own daughter talking about getting into a fight." There is no way that Sir Edward, a man of noble birth, would substitute "me" for "my".
    8. Pg 127 "There were smaller animals too, like hares and foxes, and no doubt plenty of mice to temp the owls." Or possibly to tempt them.
    9. Pg 132 "The doctor whipped around, holding up his lantern." Funny, I thought the Doctor was holding the lantern.
    10. Pg 134 "He's going to have some questions to answer when I catch up with him. And I'll take great delight in beating the replies out of him." Okay, it's not technically a cock-up, but the Doctor is going to beat replies out of Ross? And enjoy it? The Doctor?!?
    11. Pg 224 "Put on a big lock and think the door's imprega-blooming-ble." Except that if you remove "blooming", that leaves "impregable", which isn't a word.
    12. Pg 259 "Sarah pushed off from the side and swam out to join her new friends." Except in Death to the Daleks she said she couldn't swim.

    PLUGGING THE HOLES [Fan-wank theorizing of how to fix continuity cock-ups]

    1. He was shot at once, and then he was also shot.
    2. The sound is so unmistakeable it causes everyone to stutter.
    3. She's lying on her back on some sort of inflatable mattress.
    4. Fullbright was about to say something else, but then decided to end his question there.
    5. Whatever it was, it's irrelevant. (Amusingly, the very next line repeats the phrasing but used the correct "it's".)
    6. Sir Edward has a split personality. I'm sure it's not because he's a blithering idiot.
    7. Sir Edward wouldn't, but his alter ego Sir Edmund had working class roots.
    8. The mice only attract the owls temporarily.
    9. In that moment, the Doctor remembered his medical training and so reverted to being simply a doctor.
    10. He's never cruel or cowardly. Unless he really dislikes someone, of course.
    11. Plus, any attempt to insert a swear word with just one syllable to go is ridicul-blooming-ous.
    12. She's been practicing since, thanks to the TARDIS pool.

    IN SUMMARY - Robert Smith?
     Well, it's better than Genesys... It's the second-ever Missing Adventure, but you wouldn't know it, because it runs along like so many: lots of pointless continuity, a bland standalone adventure and regulars with a mix of recycled dialogue and vastly out of character moments. Oh, and Sarah's put into a swimsuit multiple times for reasons that can only seem smutty if you're familiar with Peel's other works. At times this is a fun read, but at other times it makes you want to tear your own fingernails out. Strangely, at the end of the day, those things roughly cancel each other out, leaving something rather forgettable. Much like most of the Missing Adventures then.
