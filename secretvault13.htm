    CONTINUITY COCK-UPS

    1. Pg 17 "Whether they were a twelve-eyed spider from Metebelis III or a short-sighted earthling, their response was always the same" Except the spiders of Metebelis III are originally from Earth, and Earth spiders don't have twelve eyes.
    2. Pg 41 "The Galactic Seed Vault is on the ice planet of Calufrax Major." The most valuable seed in the universe is kept on a planet that gets eaten by a pirate planet and converted into a crystal in 1978?
    3. Pg 211 "'She is...' Ryan caught himself before he said a Time Lord from the planet Gallifrey." Except neither fact was mentioned in Series 11, and the fam explicitly don't know where the Doctor is from in Spyfall.

    PLUGGING THE HOLES [Fan-wank theorizing of how to fix continuity cock-ups]

    1. These are their descendants, who've evolved.
    2. This is a clue that this section of the story is set in the past. Either that or it was Calufrax Minor that got eaten.
    3. Ryan presumably did some snooping around the TARDIS but is embarrassed by this and so doesn't tell the others and plays along in pretending not to know later on.

    IN SUMMARY - Robert Smith?
     This is absolutely bonkers... and incredibly fun. It's a whirlwind of a plot, with a talking begonia, a storyline ripped from the Keys of Marinus headlines, a mangled faculty running a death school and more virtual reality than you can shake a New Adventure at. Why is there a giant mole living under a Kensington estate being fed sacrifices by rich partygoers? No, reason, there just is. The regulars are well characterised and given plenty to do, the lashings of continuity aren't unwelcome, and the jokes are funny (the Head and Deputy Head had me laughing out loud). It even ends on a cliffhanger, because... who knows? But when it's this fun, who cares?
