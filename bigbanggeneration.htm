Big Bang Generation
by Gary Russell

Publisher: BBC
ISBN: 101 90581 4

     

    BASIC PLOT
     A time portal opens up in Sydney, Australia, linked to the Ancients of Time. The twelfth Doctor teams up with an archaeologist, but not the one he was expecting.

    DOCTOR
     Twelfth.

    COMPANIONS
     Bernice Summerfield.

    MATERIALISATION CIRCUIT

    PREPARATORY READING
     Royal Blood introduces the Glamour, which also appears in Deep Time, though you don't need that one for this.

    A familiarity with Bernice Summerfield and the Big Finish line would help.

    CONTINUITY COCK-UPS

    1. Pgs 15/42 The planet Legion has an almost permanent daytime (Page 15), yet doesn't revolve around a sun (Page 42). Sorry, what? This is because it's too far away from one (huh?) and hence stays still (whut?). Oh, and it's cold and perpetually dusk because the sun (which has apparently returned; we're still on Page 42) is a long way away. You know, Pluto is a long way from the sun, yet it still orbits it and would not really be said to have much of a daytime; Legion would have to be much further away that that for it not to orbit a star, so where exactly is this permanent daytime coming from? We're also told that the planet rotates very slowly (fair enough), meaning the dark side only faces the rest of the galaxy once in anyone's lifetime (Page 15). My head hurts. Please never speak of science again, Gary. Just... don't.
    2. Pg 82 "It just seemed wrong, bearing in mind Sydney probably saw enough snow in an average year to fill a teaspoon." This must be a very small teaspoon. The last year any snow fell in Sydney was 1836. This was also the only year any snow fell in Sydney. And Gary Russell lived in Sydney for several years, not a single one of which was 1836. Sheesh.
    3. Pg 82 "He looked to his right. The humongous Pyrmont footbridge, to the right, the IMAX cinema and the Friendship Garden." The Doctor is standing in Darling Harbour. The Pyrmont Bridge is on one side, with the IMAX cinema on the other. They can't both be to the right. Oh, and technically it's the Garden of Friendship, not the Friendship Garden. Ladies and gentlemen, we have a new winner for the number of cockups clustered together. That's three on this page alone.
    4. Pg 83 "It was a weird amalgam of both cultures - Darling Harbour being a prime example. It used the British spelling of 'harbour' whilst adopting American spellings for words like 'labor'. First up, Darling Harbour never adopted any spellings, American or otherwise. Second, this is incorrect: Australians spell the word "labour", with the sole exception of the Australian Labor Party (the national left-wing political party), which changed spelling in part to differentiate itself from being purely a labour party.
    5. Pg 90 "Last time I saw you, you were a bit younger and a bit shorter. But still Scottish." Except that Bernice saw the eighth Doctor in The Dying Days. Furthermore Page 62 confirms that she's seen him with more than one face, so this must take place after that story in her timeline.
    6. Pg 103 "Peter we'll be back in a few minutes. Don't let Globb and co out of our sight for a second." Surely this should be "out of your sight"?
    7. Pg 212 "Last time this happened to me, people repeating my words, it really didn't go very well for me, so if we could avoid all that." ...then what? The Doctor never finishes this clause, nor does he trail off.
    8. Pg 229 "They're down at my little underground base in the mountains, reprogramming EOIN, the computer that runs the place You'd like him." Say what?

    2. Gary's upstairs neighbours had an ice machine and played a very cruel prank every year.

    4. Unbeknownst to anyone except Gary Russell, from his years living in Australia (or so he says), Darling Harbour is sentient and adopted a spelling of the word "labor" for reasons known only to itself.

    IN SUMMARY - Robert Smith?
     Oh, what a terrible book! Even by Gary Russell standards, this is pretty dire. The idea of teaming up Bernice with the twelfth Doctor is a great one, but it's downhill after that. There are a zillion reference to Benny audios, none of which get a footnote or anything like that for the 99% of the audience who won't have heard them. The twelfth Doctor spends his time being sweet and hugging people. The "comedy" blurb on the back cover is a brief scene that barely has any relationship with the rest of the plot. The science of Legion is dumb. The geography of Sydney, a city Gary Russell claims to have not only been to but lived in, is atrocious. And the final question of when in the ridiculous trilogy is this Glamour book set... remains completely unaddressed. That says it all, really.
