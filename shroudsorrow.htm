by Tommy Donbavand

Publisher: BBC
ISBN: 0 385 34678 8

     

    BASIC PLOT
     In the aftermath of the Kennedy assassination, faces of the dead are pushing through into our world. And the Doctor isn't immune.

    DOCTOR
     Eleventh.

    We also see memory flashes of all ten previous Doctors.

    CONTINUITY COCK-UPS

    1. Pg 14 "Come on, sexy!" The Doctor called the TARDIS "sexy" in The Doctor's Wife. But only when they were alone. Here, Clara is present.
    2. Pg 16 "'Equipment can be replaced,' said the Doctor. people can't. Well, there is one planet where they can, but I'm not allowed back there Long story. Tried to get a refund on a shop-soiled Australian flight attendant without her permission." Except that the story about Tegan being an android was clearly a cover story to fool the Gravis in Frontios, not meant to be taken literally.
    3. Pg 174 "'Humans are pretty much universal,' the Doctor pointed out." Except these humans are in another galaxy and this is 1963. So how can there be humans on the planet Semtis in the Andromedean galaxy in the twentieth century?
    4. Pgs 207-208 "Slowly, he reached inside his jacket and pulled out his sonic. 'Do you know, I don't think I've ever used this for actually removing screws before...'" He used it for removing screws during its first appearance in Fury from the Deep, its second appearance in The War Games, as well as The Ark in Space and a number of other occasions.

    IN SUMMARY - Robert Smith?
     This starts off as a gripping story about grief in the wake of the Kennedy assassination. The Doctor is done well, with his manicness amusingly portrayed by describing some of his previous adventures for comic effect. And there's a journey out of left field involving people dressed as clowns that shouldn't work at all but does. So that's a great recipe for a top-notch story that should have ended the eleventh Doctor novels in style. Unfortunately, it ruins all the good work by devolving into puerile fanwank. Gasp! as all eleven Doctor appear (because of course they do). Cry! as Adric's death is revisited. Emote! at the Brigadier's funeral. Laugh! as the polarity of the neutron flow is reversed. Yawn! at the tedium of the well-worn cliches undermining what would otherwise be an excellent book. Oh well, said the Borad.
