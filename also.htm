The Also People
by Ben Aaronovitch

Publisher: Virgin
ISBN: 0 426 20456 5

     

    BASIC PLOT
     The Doctor and company relax inside a Dyson sphere, where an incredibly advanced race known as The People, goverened by a computer called God, are faced with the ultimate transgression: there may be a murderer in their midst. Worse than that, it appears one of the Doctor's old friends has turned up and she's the most dangerous of all...

    DOCTOR
     Seventh.

    COMPANIONS
     Benny, Chris and Roz. And Wolsey briefly (pg 286).

    MATERIALISATION CIRCUIT
     Pg 7 A clearing in a forest, in the WorldSphere of the People. The timezone is unspecified, but later books place this contemporaneous with the Benny NAs, so sometime in the late 26th century.

    In the clearing again, since the TARDIS has been spending the novel one picosecond in the future.

    CONTINUITY COCK-UPS

    1. Pg 68 "One month later Roz discovered Martle taking bribes and opened up his throat with a vibroknife." Except that in Original Sin it's stated that she knifed him in the eye.
    2. Pg 91 "Old hologram chess set in the TARDIS attic, one whose pieces fought short doomed battles when they were taken. Played a game against Melanie, tinny little voices screaming in triumph and pain. Got so distracted that I lost the match. " In Love and War, the Doctor claims never to have lost at chess until he plays Bernice.
    3. Pg 182 "They represented such a force, he knew, that if the Rutons or the Sontarans had even suspected their existence they would have climbed into the deepest darkest hole they could find and seal themselves in." Whoever the "Rutons" are, it's odd that they're mentioned in the same breath as the Sontarans, who are more normally associated with the Rutans.
    4. Pg 232 "Moire had said that, Moire the Dalek killer." Except that the Dalek Killer in Love and War was Maire (with an accented a), not Moire.

    PLUGGING THE HOLES [Fan-wank theorizing of how to fix continuity cock-ups]

    1. The flashback drink doesn't work perfectly on human biology.
    2. The Doctor was manipulating Bernice into becoming his new companion at the time. And besides, if you'd lost to Mel, you'd lie about it too, wouldn't you?
    3. The Doctor is thinking of an entirely unconnected race called the Rutons. Presumably they're at war with the Snotarans, of Adrian Rigelsford fame.
    4. Benny once knew another Dalek killer coincidentally named Moire.

    FEATURED ALIEN RACES
     The People, who consist of a variety of alien races, drones and ships. They tend to absorb alien cultures into their own (including the insect race they previously fought a war with) and their biology is mutable, so they come in all shapes and sizes.

    Pgs 8-9 An Indigenous Terran (Silurian) and the Builders, an insect-like race, in flashback.

    FEATURED LOCATIONS
     The WorldSphere of the People, a Dyson sphere consisting of continents, the planet Whynot and various space in between. Also nearby space and onboard a number of ships.

    IN SUMMARY - Robert Smith?
     Fully deserving of its place at the pinnacle of the NAs, this is everything the books can and should be: clever, funny, touching and mind-boggling. Written to perfection, with detailed characterisation for all the regulars, worldbuilding that actually feels real and some truly laugh-out-loud jokes, there's not a step out of place. This is the perfect justification for why we all read these books in the first place. Sublime.
