    CONTINUITY COCK-UPS

    1. Pg 115 "She had drunk too much of that sweet, crystallized wine." Except Sam doesn't normally drink at all.
    2. Pg 141 "I'm probably one of the few people who knew you when you were in that very first incarnation of yours. Newly on the run from Gallifrey. So young. So impetuous. Your hair not even white yet. You looked younger then than you do now." But Lungbarrow makes it clear that the Doctor didn't leave Gallifrey - at least not 'on the run' - until he looked like Susan's grandfather, and had white hair.
    3. Pg 175 "In the first room there is a yellow waxed floor and a glass roof and twelve painted doors, each at a separate o-clock." Which is odd, in a world with a 21-hour day.
    4. Pg 248 "'Bless her heart!' yelled Iris. 'She's found us!' Emerging from the shallows, and with sea water streaming from her windows, leaving her a clean and glorious crimson, came the number twenty-two to Putney Common." Iris's bus has conveniently followed her across half the world, somehow, driving through the sea just to be with her mistress. So, why didn't she do this at the beginning of the book, when her beloved mistress was trapped down a hole?

    PLUGGING THE HOLES [Fan-wank theorizing of how to fix continuity cock-ups]

    1. She didn't realise it was alcoholic when she drank it.
    2. As in many cases, Iris is being creative with the truth.
    3. The narrative is describing it in our terms; in terms of the Empress's point of view, it's just a room divided into twelve.
    4. The bus knew it couldn't rescue her, suspected that the Doctor would turn up, take it to Iris and they could be happily reunited. Damned clever these old TARDISes.

    FEATURED ALIEN RACES
     It's a veritable pot-pourri of cultures and races, so many and various are mentioned and described including:

    The ones we've heard of: Draconians, Spiridons, Martians, ambling tortoises (probably Chelonians)

    The ones we haven't heard of: sharklike bipeds, yacanas, viridian-fleshed lizard women, Djinn (corpse-eating ghouls), Kabakaj (insect-controlling spirits), a steigertrude (with little tusks), and a Sambehart.

    Native Hysperons have long, solemn faces and beige flesh tones.

    FEATURED LOCATIONS
     Hyspero, in the third decade of the Abbasid era. We visit the capital city, Hyspero; a desert with a deep pit; a caf in the middle of said desert; the town of Fortalice [a word which means 'defensive structure or position']; tunnels beneath the ground; the forest of Kesthaven; a lift to the level below the surface, which itself is vast ice fields; a beach; on the decks of the ship Kristeva; within an enormous fish; the Scarlet Empress's palace; the extreme North of the planet.

    IN SUMMARY - Anthony Wilson
     Some of the ideas and set-pieces are grand and grandiose but, by God, I wish it all hung together like a piece of whole cloth, rather than as a tapestry sewn by 27 mad nuns from different time periods. It's trying desperately hard to be about something, but in order to do so, it sacrifices anything approaching a plot beyond 'Go get this. Ah you got it. Well done.' As you meander through the narrative, you are gripped with a desperate desire to piece it all together, to see through the subtlety and the cunning of the words and understand it all. You feel that you must have missed something because you can't work it out. And then you realize, in horror, that it means nothing.
