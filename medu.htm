The Medusa Effect
by Justin Richards

Publisher: Virgin
ISBN: 0 426 20524 3

     

    BASIC PLOT
     It's as if the Marie Celeste came back. And the ghosts of the people who vanished started taking over the investigating team. Cue Benny's involvement and what transpires to be quite a cute love story.

    CONTINUITY REFERENCES
     Pg 3 "While St Oscar's is generally open and public with its research and academic work, the Advanced Research Department is a closed, secretive environment." The mysterious Advanced Research Department is part of an ongoing plotline in the Benny NAs which plays out many books from here. It was first dropped into the chronology in Mean Streets.

    Pg 4 "She rode past on her augmented bicycle, her newly complete Mark 2 as she thought of it." The Mark 1 was destroyed in Mean Streets.

    Reference to the Dalek War, albeit not by name, from Frontier in Space.

    Pg 12 "Probably Skutloid had watched the Stanturus Inquiry, where she and Braxiatel had shared the limelight." Dragons' Wrath, which is also the source of the reference to Gamaliel earlier on the page.

    Pg 32 "My department is being well paid, and I doubt it would occur to Divsen Follett that I am actually earning any of that." Follett first appeared in Dragons' Wrath.

    Pg 79 "Apart from the discovery of a bottle of vintage Eridanean brandy.' This particular vintage goes back at least as far as Shakedown.

    Pg 150 "The sugar? Or salt?" Checking for food contamination in these places brings back a swift memory of The Moonbase.

    Pg 216 "You killed Jarl Kedrick." Kind of. Mean Streets.

    Pg 238 "Braxiatel had shifted a small plaque that declared he was Keeper of the Library of St John the Beheaded." All-Consuming Fire.

    IN SUMMARY - Anthony Wilson
     It's mostly fun, and has quite a nice resolution, but the balance is way off. The plot resolves itself very quickly - you know what's happening, if not quite why, by Page 85 - and the rest, well, it takes its time. That said, it packs the occasional emotional punch and Benny herself is on top form. Very Justin Richards - indeed, most Benny NA authors were delivering a package of their quintessential style around this time, frequently on autopilot, as this threatens to be - but nonetheless quite charming in places.
