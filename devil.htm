The Devil Goblins From Neptune
by Keith Topping and Martin Day

Publisher: BBC
ISBN: 0 563 40564 3

     

    BASIC PLOT
     When a mysterious space craft crashed to Earth, several governments race to make contact with the alien race inside. However, the Doctor discovers that the craft is inhabited by the Waro, a race who's only intention is utter destruction and war.

    DOCTOR
     Third.

    COMPANIONS
     Liz Shaw, the Brigadier, Sergeant Benton, Captain Yates.

    MATERIALISATION CIRCUIT
     None. The TARDIS remains inoperable, located in the Doctor's laboratory at UNIT HQ (page 15).

    PREPARATORY READING
     None.

    PLUGGING THE HOLES [Fan-wank theorizing of how to fix continuity cock-ups]

    1. Ralph Cornish was quite young in Ambassadors, so the "Professor Cornish" in Scales might have been his father.
    2. The Earth just happened to be invaded by two similar-looking aliens in the same general area in the forties, so Control naturally thought the events of First Frontier were people talking about the Nedenah.

    IN SUMMARY - Robert Smith?
     There's a lot of fun to be had here, with a continent-spanning UNIT conspiracy, a spot-on third Doctor in his element, and some well developed aliens in the Waro. Unfortunately, there's also a plot that goes haywire, the sheer awfulness that is soul-catching, the dumbness that is the alt.universe Beatles, the Topping-ised butch and heterosexual Mike Yates (no, really!) and a deus ex machina ending where a second set of aliens conveniently turns up to sort everything out while the Doctor and UNIT stand on the sidelines and do nothing. A polished and enjoyable first half is let down by the dismal second half. That it doesn't collapse entirely is something of a minor miracle. Not awful, but it could have been so much better.
