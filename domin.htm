Dominion
by Nick Walters

Publisher: BBC
ISBN: 0 563 55574 2

     

    BASIC PLOT
     The TARDIS is ruptured and damaged, forced to close itself off to recuperate. Fitz and the Doctor end up in Sweden, where a Professor Nagle is opening a wormhole to another Universe. Meanwhile Sam ends up in that Universe, and is forced to watch as it starts to die.

    DOCTOR
     Eighth.

    COMPANIONS
     Sam Jones and Fitz Kreiner.

    CONTINUITY COCK-UPS

    1. Pg 87 "Fitz had changed out of his ruined silk shirt and was now wearing one of Bjorn's, a blue-and-black checked lumberjack thing. It felt thick and as heavy as a jacket in the humid evening. For once, however, Fitz was past caring how he looked." OK, it's a bit picky, but there are two different senses here. First, Fitz appears to be suffering from heat problems with his newly-acquired clothing, and then suddenly, for little rhyme or reason, he's not being concerned about how it looks. If you summarised, it would read 'Fitz was really hot in his clothes, but no matter, he didn't care about his appearance' which actually makes no run-on sense.
    2. Pg 101 "After two years apart, they'd ended up arguing." Now that's a really weird thing for Sam to say as, from her point of view, it was only about a week. Since she's considering her own perspective on events at this point, it's, yeah, it's damned odd.
    3. Pg 138 Sam's been rescued and, of all the questions that she could possibly ask once she can communicate, she asks "How did you rescue me?" Not 'Who are you?', 'Where am I?' or even the more courteous 'What's your name?' This is a case of the author needing a character to say something so that he can get the information across, even though it makes little sense in terms of that character and her needs and motivations.
    4. Pg 263 When the Doctor says "I didn't plan it this way at all," the words 'this way' are redundant.
    5. Pg 264 "'Sir!' cried the Schofield." Whoops.

    PLUGGING THE HOLES [Fan-wank theorizing of how to fix continuity cock-ups]

    1. As he says on the subsequent page, Fitz is tired. Presumably he's just getting a little muddled in his thought-processes.
    2. Sam's just so good at understanding and empathising with others that she instantly takes Fitz's part of the argument rather than hers. What a girl! And not at all out of character for her.
    3. Sam's scientific curiosity has been piqued. It's just the first thing that came into her head.
    4. OK, that was harsh of us too, but ultimately fair.
    5. Obviously the narrator is suffering some sort of shock as a result of the whip-lashing of the wormhole back to Earth. Or someone noticed that 'cried the Private' was unfortunate, changed the title to the name but neglected to erase the definite article, as it were.

    FEATURED ALIEN RACES
     The occupants of the Dominion come in a variety of shapes and sizes, all seemingly insect-based. They include the T'hiili, their Queen and the warrior T'vorha. Other beings include the Ruin and the Bane, neither of which are all that pleasant.

    FEATURED LOCATIONS
     A lake, a farmhouse, a forest and the C19 Underground base, around five miles from Strangnas in Southern Sweden, 31st June 1999 AD. Also the town of Strangnas itself, about 50 miles West of Stockholm. The hospital there is the Royal Infirmary of Eskilstuna.

    The Dominion, resembling an underground location, but actually an entire pocket universe in its own right. By the end of the novel, it has been destroyed.

    IN SUMMARY - Anthony Wilson
     You can't fault the imagination of the setting, but it's damned hard to keep track of which type of alien is which. The structure's all over the shop as well: Sam spends forever exploring the Dominion and we're given no explanations for what feel like decades, which is supposed to be fantastic (in the old sense), but ends up being deathly dull. Similarly, the Doctor doesn't enter the action in the Dominion for a massive 247 pages, and when he does, all he can contribute is an escape route and pure technobabble. On the bright side, the structure means that we are spared 100 pages of Sam, so maybe we shouldn't complain. By far the funniest thing, though, is that, when the Doctor loses the TARDIS, he's supposed to come over all useless. So our question is this: how we were supposed to tell the difference from his normal state?
