    BASIC PLOT
     The TARDIS lands the Doctor and Martha in the Lake District in 1909, where a small village has been terrorised by a giant, scaly monster. The search is on for the elusive 'Beast of Westmorland', and explorers, naturalists and hunters from across the country are descending on the fells. King Edward VII himself is on his way to join the search, with a knighthood for whoever finds the Beast.

    IN SUMMARY - Robert Smith?
     This is light and fluffy and rather gorgeous. It's not aiming to be anything other than a fun runaround, with an enormous number of bait and switches about exactly who's a Zygon, but it succeeds admirably. The period setting is perfect for Zygon wackiness, although having more than one Skarasen was probably a mistake. The black and white characters on the cover are a trifle odd, but not necessarily unpleasant. All up, precisely the sort of thing to devour on a rainy afternoon. If it takes you any longer, you're doing something wrong...
