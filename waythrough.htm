    IN SUMMARY - Robert Smith?
     This is quite fantastic. The opening is especially good, with an effectively creepy setup and a timey-wimey explanation of the setting that works marvellously. The characters are very well-drawn and the action zooms by. The Doctor gets sidelined in jail for most of the action and finally springs forth, solving the problem in about five minutes, but you don't really mind because it's all so engaging. It falls apart a little towards the end, as Reyne turns suddenly evil for no reason and the Ship as Harry spends way too much time blubbering. But then the resolution is very nice indeed. Highly recommended.
