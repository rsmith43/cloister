    CONTINUITY COCK-UPS

    1. Pg 18 "That particular invitation had been to an interspecieal diplomatic gathering on Luna in 1609 - by the Gregorian calendar - and it had resulted in nothing but trouble." This is clearly The Empire of Glass... except that that story took palce on Laput, not Luna.
    2. Pg 211 "Outside the standing Zone of Illusion that had been maintined for the incursive troops, the landscape of Moriel showed its true and baleful face." Say what?
    3. Pg 220 "In contrastto the seamless finish of the conrridor outside, they were finished with rough and mismatched steel plate." Huh? "contrastto" is bad enough, but surely there should be more than one steel plate?
    4. Pg 233 "His voice was drowned out by the machine, but the Doctor , standing by it, seemd to hear every word" Wha-?
    5. Pg 247 "'And I never noticed,' said the doctor angrily." Why isn't he capitalised?
    6. Pg 255 "'Well, obviously, I never denied - ' Sareth began. 'Then he suddenly turned to stare off to one side. 'Doctor? Where are you going?'" Why is there an extra quote mark after Sareth begins?
    7. Pgs 264-265 "It's paw hovered over the console" Sould that be "Its paw"?

    PLUGGING THE HOLES [Fan-wank theorizing of how to fix continuity cock-ups]

    1. It's been a long time for the Doctor, so he's misremembering.
    2. The illusion forks your memory into fragments, so it is consequently "maintined". (Sorry.)
    3. The number of steel plates is the contrastto. It's a design term, intended to throw you out of your comfort zone.
    4. The Doctor has gone all Shakespearean for a moment and thus seem'd to hear every word, only in iambic pentameter.
    5. That's how angry he is.
    6. Sareth isn't quite sure where he's beginning.
    7. The paw isn't possessive. It really is meant to be "It is paw hovered over the console." Must be an alien expression.

    IN SUMMARY - Robert Smith?
     This is a frustrating mix of some very nice parts and some very frustrating ones. Dave Stone's inability to use one word where ten will do actively plays against the books strengths, drowning some nice plotting in excess verbiage. Roz and Chris are given absolutely nothing to do, which is criminal. However, Benny and Jason's touching romance is a highlight, while the subtly menacing and everpresent nature of the Doctor is incredible. The reveal of the true villains is well done. And the Doctor's utterly brilliant solution at the end - a three way marriage between the three leaders of the three empires (two male and one female) is wholly unexpected, perfectly apt and downright genius.
