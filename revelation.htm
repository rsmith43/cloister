    BASIC PLOT
     Many years ago, young Dorothy was killed in the playground by her schoolyard torturer, Chad Boyle. Now, the Timewyrm has teamed Lt Hemmings with Boyle so they can do it all over again. A sentient church and its occupants are transported to the moon after the destruction of Cheldon Bonniface. In a surreal datascape inhabited by a number of very familiar figures, it looks like the Timewyrm might finally be winning as she seeks to erase Ace from history and finally overcome the Doctor. Who's going to save them this time?

    CONTINUITY COCK-UPS

    1. Pg 10 "Ace was in her early twenties". Not in the last three books she wasn't. Nor will she be in Cat's Cradle: Time's Crucible, where she's 18.
    2. Pg 30 Lt Hemmings' first name changes from Anthony in Timewyrm: Exodus to Rupert here.
    3. Pg 30 "If she had been awake, Ace would have wondered why her childhood bully was still only eight years old." Actually, given that Ace knows that she is a time-traveller fighting a time-travelling villain who has access to people's memories, it's highly unlikely that Ace would have wondered any such thing.
    4. Pg 74 "The blast obliterated the Norfolk village of Cheldon Bonniface, the destruction extending to certain parts of Wroxham and nearby Stockbridge." In its first appearance, in DWM#68, Stockbridge was in Gloucestershire, which is nowhere near Norfolk.
    5. Pg 124 "'And this is - this is different. Ah, Mrs Trelaw -' 'Please.' Emily grinned up at the rafters. 'Call me Emily.'" For good reason, because Emily is Mrs Hutchings, whilst the Reverend Trelaw is not married. Oops.
    6. Pg 170 "Birth thoughts blossomed around her, and Emily's tears streamed at the closeness of a mother's breast." This seems to imply the Doctor had a mother and was breastfed, which would contradict Cat's Cradle: Time's Crucible and Lungbarrow

    PLUGGING THE HOLES [Fan-wank theorizing of how to fix continuity cock-ups]

    1. The Doctor's messing about with Ace's memories in Genesys has left her with the side-effect that she keeps misremembering her age. This only settles down once she leaves the Doctor.
    2. Happy Endings reveals that his full name is Anthony Rupert Hemmings.
    3. The narrative is assuming that Ace would be confused, which is a reasonable assumption in the circumstances.
    4. There might be two Stockbridges or the Timewyrm's rewriting of reality could have caused the confusion.
    5. Saul (yes, the ancient, knowledgeable entity that's been around for over a thousand years) gets confused. Ah well.
    6. Emily might be translating the Doctor's experiences into her own frame of reference.

    IN SUMMARY - Robert Smith?
     The cornerstone of every Doctor Who book that followed, Timewyrm: Revelation is nothing short of brilliant. The NAs burst into the limelight with its publication, but it's also a fantastic read in its own right. There's an emotional journey here, written with subtlety and intelligence that takes apart the very concept of Doctor Who and put in back together again. Breathtaking.
