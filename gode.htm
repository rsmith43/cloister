    PREPARATORY READING
     This book is nominally a sequel to Transit (it starts as that book ends), but there aren't that many references. More important is to be familiar with The Dalek Invasion of Earth, Pyramids of Mars and The Seeds of Death. The Curse of Peladon and Happy Endings wouldn't go astray either.

    IN SUMMARY - Robert Smith?
     You might as well call it GodAwful. Because it really is. The continuity overload is bad enough; it's a million times worse following Happy Endings. The attempts to be a sequel to Transit are a failure. The plot's a mess: two thirds of the novel involves people wandering around Osirian-derived Egpytian iconography of Ice Warrior architecture, commenting on how they wish Benny was here. The plot only really gets going once everyone's in the same room, but we have to wait forever for that to happen. And the soap opera Ice Warriors are just ridiculous. For the most part, the writing is at least fine, but it does contain its fair share of clunkers (eg "One didn't have to be a genius to work out what was going on, even though she was" from page 131 or "She and Rachel had been unstrapped from their beds by Yeess - none too gently, come to that, although what had she expected? Hotel service and a massage - and escorted from the 'waiting room' to yet another welcoming chamber carved from solid rock, presumably the location of the Martians' precious Brain-rack" from page 167). There are rumours that this was written in a hurry. You can tell.
