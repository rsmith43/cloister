by Andy Lane and Jim Mortimore

Publisher: Virgin
ISBN: 0 426 20388 7

     

    BASIC PLOT
     The Doctor, Ace and Benny travel to an orbital base around the planet Lucifer, the site of an expedition trying to communicate with the Angels who live there. Investigating sabotage, the Doctor discovers the Interplanetary Mining Corporation (IMC) to be behind it, sending an extradimensional alien named Legion to take over the base. But just who is IMC's contact on the base?

    IN SUMMARY - Robert Smith?
     Two thirds of this book is pretty much the best thing ever. Page for page, word for word, this is an astonishing novel. The characterisation is amazing, giving deep and meaningful personalities to everyone, from major players, to bit parts. The worldbuilding is incredible, with attention to detail the like of which we've rarely seen elsewhere. The regulars slot into this with aplomb. And the murder mystery holds it all together, in a neat package. Unfortunately, they didn't stop there. If they had, this would have been remembered as the single best NA bar none. Sadly, the last third of the book is filled with stomping IMC soldiers, Ace's dreary allegiance-switching and the callous off-screen murder of almost all the interesting characters who'd been so lovingly detailed to this point. The only decent thing about the final third is Legion, but he can't sustain a wounded and bleeding novelette that destroys most of the goodwill built up from the amazing first part. This is an enormous tragedy.
